# Inventory

Inventory is for players who want to carry more items, keep more valuable loot before selling or scrapping it, change equipment more cheaply, and use higher-level clothing with less power loss.

## Live Status

| Trait | Status | What it changes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Capacity | Live | Total carried item weight limit. |
| Exemption | Live | How many of the heaviest carried items are ignored before inventory weight is counted. |
| Levitation | Live | How much remaining carried item weight is reduced. |
| Equipping | Live | Essence cost paid when clothing is equipped. |
| Overlevel | Live | How much useful power remains when equipped clothing is above character level. |

Inventory affects carried items. Equipped clothing is not counted against inventory capacity, but equipped weight still affects [Stamina Relief](stamina.md#relief).

## Why Invest In Inventory

Inventory is a quality-of-life and trading attribute. It helps players:

* keep more loot before deciding what to sell or scrap;
* carry heavier high-rarity or high-level items;
* swap equipment more cheaply;
* buy and use stronger higher-level clothing earlier;
* avoid being forced to scrap or sell too soon because of weight pressure.

A player who opens many lootboxes, trades actively, or experiments with equipment builds will feel Inventory more than a player who immediately scraps most drops.

## Capacity

Capacity raises the total weight a character can carry in inventory.

This is the broadest Inventory trait: it helps with every carried item, whether the item is light or heavy. Item grants can add flat kilograms and can also increase capacity by a percentage.

Player impact:

* More room for lootbox drops.
* More room to hold marketplace inventory.
* Less pressure to scrap low-value items immediately.

## Exemption

Exemption ignores the heaviest carried items before the rest of inventory weight is counted.

This is strongest when a player carries a few very heavy items. It is less important when the inventory is full of many small items.

Player impact:

* Good for holding a few heavy high-level items.
* Combines well with Capacity because ignored items do not consume the weight limit.
* Combines differently from Levitation: Exemption removes whole heavy items, while Levitation makes many items lighter.

## Levitation

Levitation reduces the counted weight of carried items that were not ignored by Exemption.

This is strongest when a player carries many items. Each counted item always keeps at least a small amount of weight, so Levitation cannot make inventory weight disappear completely.

Player impact:

* Good for lootbox-heavy players with many carried items.
* Helps traders hold broader stock.
* Helps crafters keep items before choosing whether to scrap, level, or sell.

## Equipping

Equipping reduces the Essence cost of putting clothing on.

Changing gear is meant to be a real decision, not a free instant switch. Equipping makes experimentation cheaper and lets a player adjust builds more often.

Player impact:

* Cheaper gear swaps.
* Easier testing of different sets and booster links.
* Less Essence lost when upgrading the character's equipment.

Equipment changes always keep a minimum Essence cost.

## Overlevel

Characters can wear clothing above their own level. Overlevel decides how much useful item power remains when the item is too far ahead of the character.

Overlevel affects useful item values such as Aspect, attribute grants, trait perks, booster values, and direct grants. It does not reduce the item's weight, rarity, slot, set, origin, ownership, or equip cost.

Player impact:

* Makes high-level marketplace purchases useful earlier.
* Lets a lower-level character borrow more power from advanced gear.
* Creates a tradeoff: a high-level item can be powerful, but its full weight still applies.

The important idea is Level Fit: the further the item level is above the character level, the more item power can be reduced. Better Overlevel narrows that gap.
