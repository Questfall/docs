# RPG Economy

The RPG system gives Questfall an item economy around quest activity.

Without RPG items, quest mining would mostly be a direct reward loop: complete quests, receive rewards, repeat. RPG progression adds another layer: players can build different characters, value different items, trade with each other, and turn spare loot into resources.

## Why RPG Matters

RPG mechanics solve three economic problems at the same time:

* they give players more reasons to keep playing after basic quest completion;
* they create demand for tradeable items;
* they let different player strategies support each other.

A miner may want better Mining and Stamina gear. A trader may want more Slots and lower Fee. A crafter may buy spare items, scrap them, and level useful clothing. A luck-focused player may care about lootboxes and shard completion.

That variety is what makes item trading meaningful.

## Current Live Loop

The live RPG economy is centered on clothing:

```text
quest activity
-> Chest Shards and lootboxes
-> clothing items
-> equip, sell, scrap, or level up
-> stronger or more specialized character
```

Clothing can be valuable because of rarity, level, Aspect, perks, set, origin, slot, and weight. This makes item demand specific instead of generic.

## Gold, Silver, And Essence

The current RPG loop uses several resources:

| Resource | Role |
| --- | --- |
| Gold | Opens Common Lootboxes, trades marketplace items, and converts into Silver. |
| Silver | Supports character progression and other system actions. |
| Essence | Comes from scrapping items and is spent to level up clothing. |

This creates several player paths:

* questers earn progression and shard opportunities;
* lootbox openers create clothing supply;
* crafters turn spare clothing into Essence and improve selected items;
* traders move items to players who value them more.

## Planned Expansion

The broader design still includes future RPG economy surfaces such as Potions, Gems, item rarity evolution, QFT-denominated high-rarity trading, and liquidity-linked Gem rewards.

Those systems should be treated as planned until their product surfaces are live. The current live RPG economy is clothing, attributes, lootboxes, Essence, stamina, luck, and marketplace trading.
